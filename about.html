The Founder

From Lyon, with love

Céleste Dubois grew up in the Rhône-Alpes region of France, where early mornings meant the smell of her grandmother's kitchen — warm butter, yeast, and sugar browning at the edges of a battered copper pan.

She trained formally at a pastry school in Lyon before spending three years in Paris apprenticing under two Meilleur Ouvrier de France patissiers. She learned that great pastry is not about showing off — it is about restraint, about knowing when to stop, about trusting the process.

In 2017, she came home. Not to France, but to Millbrook, the small town in the valley where her late husband had roots, and where she had spent enough summers to know the quality of the light in August and the way the farmers' market smelled on Saturday mornings.

She signed a lease on a small space on Willow Lane, ordered a secondhand deck oven, and opened Maison Vale with a single sourdough loaf, a dozen croissants, and a handwritten sign in the window.

Eight years later, the lease is longer and the oven is newer, but not much else has changed. Céleste still arrives at 4am. She still shapes every loaf. She still believes that the most important ingredient in good bread is time.

What We Stand For

The things that don't change

⏳

Patience

Our croissants are laminated over two days. Our sourdough ferments for 18 hours minimum. We don't rush what shouldn't be rushed.

🌾

Ingredient Quality

French AOP butter. Heritage grain flours from a mill 40 miles north. Eggs from a farm we visit personally. Seasonal fruit from growers we know by name.

🏘

Community

Maison Vale is a neighborhood bakery, not a franchise. We know our regulars by name, their orders by heart, and the fabric of this community by daily presence.

✦

Craft

We are craftspeople before we are business people. Every score on a loaf, every layer in a croissant, every hand-piped rosette means something to us.

The Bakery

A place to slow down

42 Willow Lane is a modest space — ten small tables, a long marble counter, and large windows that let in the morning light. We designed it to feel like a living room, not a showroom.

There is no background music that competes. The sound is the sound of the bakery: the oven venting, milk being steamed, a conversation between regulars. We think that's the right kind of atmosphere.

You're welcome to sit as long as you like. Nobody will rush you.
